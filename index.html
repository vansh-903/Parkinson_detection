Parkinson disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder affecting movement. It's characterized by tremors, rigidity, slowness of movement, and impaired balance. Caused by the loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain, it leads to motor and non-motor symptoms, impacting daily life. Early detection and management are crucial for improved quality of life.

Alzheimer disease

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterized by memory loss, cognitive decline, and behavioral changes. It is the most common cause of dementia, gradually impairing a person's ability to function independently. Early detection and intervention are crucial for managing symptoms and improving quality of life.
